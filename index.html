About Me

I am a dedicated economist specializing in macroeconomics, monetary policy, international finance, financial stability, and climate economics. Currently, I am a Ph.D. candidate in Econometrics & Quantitative Economics at American University, expecting to graduate in May 2025.

I serve as a consultant at the World Bank Group, leading research on the macroeconomic impacts of climate change. My work integrates macroeconomic analysis, financial stability, and policy evaluation to enhance diagnostic tools and support policy formulation.

Previously, as a core member of the Macro-Financial Review team at the World Bank, I contributed to semi-annual reports and played a pivotal role in developing the Macro-Financial Spider Tool Model for global macro-financial risk assessment, which informed discussions with the Financial Stability Board.

I hold a Master of Public Administration from Columbia University's School of International and Public Affairs, where I studied under renowned economist Guillermo Calvo. This experience provided a solid foundation in both theoretical and applied economics.

Since May 2022, I have been actively involved in the China Economic Policy Uncertainty (EPU) Project, utilizing NLP technology for data collection and analysis to advance the understanding of economic policy uncertainty in China.

My research has been presented at international conferences and focuses on macroeconomics and financial stability. I have been honored with awards such as the Frank M. Tamagna Prize for excellence in international finance and banking.

Proficient in Python, MATLAB, Dynare, R, STATA, and other research tools, I am dedicated to using quantitative methods to solve complex economic challenges. I welcome collaboration and exchange.

About Me

I am a dedicated economist specializing in macroeconomics, monetary policy, international finance, financial stability, and climate economics. Currently, I am a Ph.D. candidate in Econometrics & Quantitative Economics at American University, expecting to graduate in May 2025. I have been a teaching assistant for courses such as International Economics, Monetary Policy, Banking and Finance, Financial Stability and Growth, and Applied Economics.

My academic journey includes a Master of Public Administration from Columbia University's School of International and Public Affairs, where I studied under the renowned economist Guillermo Calvo.

My research is deeply influenced by my work at the World Bank Group, where I currently serve as a consultant. I lead research on the macroeconomic impacts of climate change, developing assessments that integrate macroeconomic analysis with financial stability and policy evaluation, addressing frontier issues in the macro-finance of climate change. This work aims to enhance the World Bank's diagnostic tools and support policymakers in crafting effective strategies for sustainable development.

Previously, as a core member of the Macro-Financial Review team at the World Bank, I contributed to the organization's third and fourth semi-annual reports. I played a pivotal role in developing and maintaining the Macro-Financial Spider Tool Model for global macro-financial risk assessment, which has informed discussions with the Financial Stability Board.

In addition to my professional and academic pursuits, I am actively involved in the China Economic Policy Uncertainty (EPU) Project, utilizing NLP technology. Since May 2022, I have managed data collection and analysis to advance the understanding of economic policy uncertainty in China.

I have co-authored papers presented at international conferences, such as the International Conference on Management Science and Engineering Management, and published works focusing on macroeconomics and financial stability. My contributions to the field have been recognized through awards like the Frank M. Tamagna Prize for excellence in international finance and banking.

Proficient in Python, MATLAB, Dynare, R, STATA, and other research tools, I am equipped to tackle complex economic challenges using quantitative methods. I am passionate about leveraging my skills to contribute to the field of economics and welcome opportunities for collaboration.

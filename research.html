Working Papers

Assessing the Macro-Critical Impacts of Climate Change: Key Considerations for a Research Agenda
with Nepomuk Dunz and Christian Schoder
Submitted to The World Bank Research Observer, February 12, 2025 (Link)

The assessment of climate-related economic impacts and the formulation of effective policies present significant analytical challenges. This paper explores these challenges by examining the socio-economic system's key characteristics, such as uncertainty, nonlinear shock transmission, and socio-economic tipping points, which are essential for evaluating climate impacts and devising decarbonization strategies.

Policy Interventions and China’s Stock Market in the Early Stages of the COVID-19 Pandemic
with Steven J. Davis, Dingqian Liu, and Xuguang Simon Sheng
Hoover Institution Economics Working Paper 25102 (Link), NBER Working Paper 33485, DOI: 10.3386/w33485, Issue Date: February 2025 (Link)

China’s stock market greatly outperformed other national markets during the first several months of the COVID-19 pandemic, even before it became evident that early containment efforts would flounder in the United States and other countries. This study evaluates policy interventions and their effects on stock volatility and price levels.

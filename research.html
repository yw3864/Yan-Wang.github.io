Working Papers

Assessing the Macro-Critical Impacts of Climate Change: Key Considerations for a Research Agenda

with Nepomuk Dunz and Christian Schoder

Submitted to The World Bank Research Observer, February 12, 2025 (Link)

This paper explores the key macroeconomic impacts of climate change and highlights critical areas for future research. It discusses socio-economic tipping points, nonlinear climate shock transmission, and the importance of uncertainty in policy formulation. Additionally, it identifies analytical improvements needed for assessing the risks and opportunities related to decarbonization policies.

with Steven J. Davis, Dingqian Liu, and Xuguang Simon Sheng

Hoover Institution Economics Working Paper 25102 (Link), NBER Working Paper 33485, DOI: 10.3386/w33485, Issue Date: February 2025 (Link)

This study analyzes China's stock market outperformance during early COVID-19 and evaluates the impact of monetary, credit, and equity market interventions. It finds evidence of increased stock volatility but limited effects on stock price levels.
